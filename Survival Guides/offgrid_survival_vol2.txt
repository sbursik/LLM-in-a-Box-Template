OFF-GRID MEDICAL FIELD HANDBOOK

PRIMARY MEDICAL PRIORITIES:

Airway Breathing Circulation Infection Prevention

TRAUMA RESPONSE:

Stop bleeding: Direct pressure, tourniquet if severe.

Clean wounds: Clean water, antiseptic if available.

Immobilize fractures: Splints, minimize movement.

INFECTION CONTROL:

Hand hygiene critical. Boil instruments when possible. Isolate
contagious individuals.

COMMON OFF-GRID ILLNESSES:

Waterborne disease Respiratory infection Skin infections
Malnutrition-related illness

CHRONIC CONDITION MANAGEMENT:

Stock essential medications. Monitor diet closely. Maintain stress
control.

PREVENTION SAVES MORE LIVES THAN TREATMENT.
OFF-GRID COMMUNICATIONS MANUAL

RADIO TYPES:

AM/FM broadcast radios FRS/GMRS handheld radios HAM radio systems
Shortwave receivers

POWER SOURCES:

Solar charging Hand-crank generators Battery storage

SIGNALING METHODS:

Visual: Mirrors, flags, lights.

Audible: Whistles, horns, coded signals.

Written: Bulletin boards, message drops.

COMMUNICATION SECURITY:

Avoid broadcasting sensitive information. Use plain language clarity.

INFORMATION FLOW:

Reliable communication stabilizes communities.
FOOD INDEPENDENCE GUIDE

SOIL HEALTH:

Compost organic matter. Ensure proper drainage. Rotate crops annually.

CROP BASICS:

Staples: Potatoes, beans, grains, squash.

Fast yield: Leafy greens, radishes.

PROTEIN SOURCES:

Fishing Small livestock Legumes

FOOD PRESERVATION:

Drying Smoking Fermentation Root cellaring

SEED SAVING:

Preserve best plants. Store cool, dry, dark.

Agriculture stability equals long-term survival.
