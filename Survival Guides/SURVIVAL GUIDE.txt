Below is a master checklist of critical questions organized by domain. This is framed for preparedness, resilience, and continuity of life in the event of government collapse, systemic failure, or a post-apocalyptic environment. The emphasis is on decision-making, risk reduction, and long-term survivability, not tactics for harming others.

1. Situational Awareness & Reality Assessment

What exactly failed: government, utilities, supply chains, currency, or all of the above?

Is this collapse localized, regional, national, or global?

Is the situation temporary, cyclical, or permanent?

What information sources are still reliable?

Who controls communication channels, if anyone?

What rumors are circulating—and what evidence contradicts them?

What is changing daily vs. hourly?

2. Personal Safety & Immediate Threats

What are the primary threats right now (violence, exposure, starvation, disease)?

Where is it unsafe to travel—and why?

Are people organizing for protection or predation?

What signals indicate escalating danger?

How visible should I be?

What defensive measures are lawful, ethical, and sustainable?

How do I de-escalate conflict instead of creating it?

3. Shelter & Environment

Where can I safely shelter long-term?

Is my shelter defensible, concealed, and structurally sound?

How vulnerable is it to fire, flood, weather, or human intrusion?

What natural resources exist nearby?

What environmental hazards exist (radiation, toxins, wildlife)?

What is my backup shelter if this one fails?

How long will it last?

Final Thought

The most dangerous phase of collapse is not scarcity—it is confusion, panic, and moral collapse. The people who survive longest are not the most aggressive, but the most adaptive, cooperative, disciplined, and ethically grounded.
